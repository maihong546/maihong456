WHAT IS IT ABOUT?

This task analyses an interactive web project from the course modules, focusing on user interaction and personal experience. The selected website, New Legibility, offers a non-linear and dynamic reading environment where users engage through actions such as clicking and dragging. This response evaluates how the experience communicates its purpose and shapes user engagement.

FIRST IMPRESSION and INTERACTION FLOW

When I first visited New Legibility, what immediately drew my attention was the unconventional layout and the moving text elements scattered across the screen. Rather than following a fixed structure, the content appeared dynamically, giving the interface an experimental and non-linear feel. My initial reaction was a mix of curiosity and slight disorientation, as it defied my usual expectations of how a website should behave.

During the first two minutes, I mainly interacted by clicking on different text blocks, dragging elements around, and scrolling to uncover hidden or newly revealed content. Each action produced a response, text shifting or appearing in unexpected places which encouraged me to keep exploring. The experience felt less like reading a traditional article and more like navigating an interactive system.

I spent the most time with the draggable text elements, which allowed me to rearrange content and craft my own reading path. This design encourages active participation, letting users construct meaning rather than passively consuming information. The most repeated action was clicking, as it consistently revealed new pieces of text and guided the flow of the experience.

PURPOSE and COMMUNICATION

The primary goal of New Legibility is to challenge traditional reading conventions and explore how meaning can be actively constructed through interaction. Unlike standard websites with a linear, predictable flow, this project transforms text into dynamic elements that users can manipulate, making reading a participatory and exploratory process. It encourages users to think critically about the act of reading itself and to engage with content in a way that is both playful and reflective.

This goal is communicated through multiple interactive cues. The scattered and movable text blocks immediately signal that the user should click, drag, or rearrange content. Visual feedback, such as text shifting, appearing in new locations, or responding to clicks, reinforces that interaction is not only expected but central to understanding the material. The absence of traditional navigation or a clearly defined start-to-finish sequence further emphasizes that personal exploration and experimentation are key components of the experience. In essence, the design itself teaches users how to engage.

TIME BASED EXPERIENCE and GUIDANCE

New Legibility is designed to be explored over multiple short sessions rather than consumed in a single sitting. Its non-linear, unpredictable nature encourages users to revisit the site, experiment with different text arrangements, and uncover new relationships between content. There is no prescribed duration for interaction; instead, the website rewards repeated engagement, allowing users to construct their own rhythm and pace of exploration.

The site communicates this temporal approach through its interactive mechanics. Clickable and draggable text elements, combined with immediate visual feedback, subtly indicate that the experience is iterative. Animations and responsive changes guide users intuitively, without explicit instructions, showing that understanding grows through repetition and experimentation. By allowing users to interact freely and observe the consequences of their actions, the experience fosters curiosity and reflection, suggesting that engagement over time is essential to fully grasp the concept of non-linear reading.

REFERENCE, EMOTIONS and EVALUATION

The website references other interactive media forms, such as experimental digital art, generative typography, and playful game-like interfaces. These references suggest a mode of engagement that is exploratory and creative rather than passive. Users are invited to act like participants or co-creators rather than simple readers, experimenting with the placement and arrangement of text to uncover meaning.

Emotionally, the experience evokes a mixture of curiosity, excitement, and occasional frustration. The most satisfying aspect for me was interacting with the draggable text elements. Being able to rearrange content and craft my own reading path created a sense of agency and control, which made the exploration highly rewarding. Conversely, the most frustrating part was when the text overlapped or shifted too rapidly, making it temporarily difficult to follow and slightly disorienting. This tension, however, also reflects the goal of the website and the challenging conventional reading habits, turning discomfort into a productive aspect of the learning experience.

Overall, New Legibility succeeds in creating an immersive, interactive environment where users actively construct meaning. Its playful mechanics, iterative design, and experimental layout provoke reflection on how we engage with text, demonstrating that interactive media can transform reading from a passive activity into an engaging, participatory experience.
